About me

Hi, I'm Piyush Sahoo — a curious builder from India, deeply fascinated by Artificial Intelligence, Machine learning, Python, and the art of turning ideas into practical & working systems.

I grew up loving the "how" behind things: How does a model actually learns patterns from data? How can a small script automate hours of manual work? How do open-source tools let anyone experiment with state-of-the-art ideas without a massive budget? That curiosity eventually pulled me into the world of AI and engineering full-time.

This website — Piyush Writes — is my public notebook. Here I document the things I'm learning and building:



  • Clear, no-fluff breakdowns of machine learning concepts (starting from the basics and going deeper when it makes sense)
  • Step-by-step guides to real projects — the ones that actually ship, and the ones that teach the most when they crash and burn
  • Honest thoughts on new papers, tools, frameworks, and techniques I'm experimenting with
  • Reflections on the engineering mindset: what works in practice, what sounds good in theory but fails in production, and how to keep learning without getting overwhelmed


Right now, the site is small — just a couple of deep-dive posts, but that's intentional. I'm not rushing to fill pages; I'm focusing on writing pieces I'd want to read myself months later. The How-to Guides, Experiments, and more sections are coming as I finish projects worth sharing.

When I'm not coding or writing, you'll find me:


<'br>
  • Reading the latest arXiv papers (and trying to implement the interesting ones)
  • Playing with new open-source stacks — sometimes just to see what breaks
  • Talking to other builders who geek out over the same problems
  • Occasionally stepping away to recharge with non-tech things (because balance matters, even if the next idea is itching to get out)


I'm based in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, and a lot of my perspective comes from that context: limited resources forcing creative solutions, the excitement of open communities, and the drive to build things that matter locally while thinking globally.



If any of this resonates — if you're also grinding through tutorials, debugging at 2 AM, celebrating small wins, or just trying to make sense of the fast-moving AI world — I'd love to connect.

Feel free to reach out if you have questions about AI/ML/engineering, want to chat about a paper/tool, have collaboration ideas, or just want to share your own journey. No gatekeeping here.



Thanks for stopping by. More writings (and experiments) on the way.

— Piyush
